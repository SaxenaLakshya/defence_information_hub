Modern Military Weapons

T-72

T-72 Ajeya and its MK2 variant, sourced from the Soviet Union and manufactured in India, are currently in service. Plans are underway to replace them with the Future Ready Combat Vehicle (FRCV) and Future Main Battle Tank, with upgrades ongoing to enhance their capabilities.

Nag Missiles

For tank destroyers, the NAMICA, based on the BMP-2 chassis and carrying Nag missiles, is in service with orders for additional units. Production is jointly undertaken by Ordnance Factory Medak and L&T.

BMP-2

BMP-2 "Sarath," manufactured locally in India by Ordnance Factory Medak, remains in service. Efforts are underway to upgrade all BMP-2/2K vehicles to the BMP-2M standard, with an initial contract signed for upgrades.

CRBN Reconnaissance Vehicle

CRBN reconnaissance vehicle, and the Carrier Mortar Tracked, a mortar carrier, both based on the BMP-2 platform and manufactured by Ordnance Factory Medak.

MPi-KMS-72

MPi-KMS-72 from East Germany and the Vz. 58 from Czechoslovakia remain in service, reflecting the diversity of weapons used by the Indian Army.

INSAS 1B1

INSAS 1B1 Assault Rifle, chambered in 5.56×45mm, has been in service but is slated for replacement by the AK-203 and SIG 716i.

FN SCAR L and FN SCAR H

the FN SCAR L and FN SCAR H, both in service and used by special forces. The IWI Tavor X95, a bullpup assault rifle manufactured locally in India, and the IMI Tavor TAR-21, standard issue for Special Forces like Para SF, Garud, and MARCOS, are also in service.

M4 carbine

M4 carbine, sourced from the United States, while the T91 assault rifle from Taiwan has been acquired with 400 units in service. The AR-M1, chambered in 7.62×39mm and modified locally by Israel's FAB Defense, and the AKM and AK-47, both in service and modified locally by SSS Defence India and FAB Defense, are also utilized by the Army.
